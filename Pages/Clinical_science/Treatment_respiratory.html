Types of medication in respiratory medicine

For the treatment of the most common respiratory conditions, we know three classes of medication:

  1. Beta2-agonists
  2. Muscarinic antagonists
  3. Corticosteroids

Importantly, besides these, there are so-called phosphodiesterase-4 (PDE4) inhibitors which are used in patients with severe COPD with frequent exacerbations. An important example thereof is Roflumilast.

Bronchodilators

There are two kinds of bronchodilators, namely Beta2-agonists and muscarinic antagonists. While both help to reduce airway spasms, they work through different mechanisms. Both, however, are usually known by their abbreviations:

  1. Short-acting Beta2-agonists (SABA): E.g., Salbutamol
  2. Long-acting Beta2-agonists (LABA): E.g., Salmeterol, Formoterol
  3. Short-acting Muscarinic Antagonists (SAMA): E.g., Ipratropium
  4. Long-Acting Muscarinic Antagonists (LAMA): E.g., Tiotropium

Beta2-agonists

Beta-receptors are a type of adrenergic receptor i.e., receptors that respond to (nor)epinephrine. There are three types of beta-receptors:

  1. Beta1-receptors: Found in the heart and kidneys and have a chrono-, ino- and dromotropic function; in the kidneys, they stimulate renin release
  2. Beta2-receptors: found in the airways, blood vessels (esp. in skeletal muscle) and gastrointestinal tract; and they cause broncho- and vasodilation and relaxation of smooth muscle in general
  3. Beta3-receptors: Found in adipose tissue and the bladder; functions to stimulate lipolysis and relax the bladder

With higher dosages, the "fight-or-flight" system overactives and one might experience tremors, tachycardia, palpitations and a headache

Imagine being called a beta! You adrenaline would put you in fight mode, so increased cardiovascular function, increased respiratory efficiency, more blood to your muscles, increased energy from fat etc. Basically all the stereotypical sympathetic nervous system function!
Most beta2-agonists end in terol

Muscarinic antagonists

From our studies in neurology, we know that there are 3 types of muscarinic receptor subtypes:

  1. M1: Found in the CNS and parasympathetic ganglia, these stimulate cholinergic transmission
  2. M2: Found in the heart and lungs, these inhibit acetylcholine release via a feedback loop
  3. M3: Found in smooth muscle and glands, these stimulate bronchoconstriction and mucus secretion

The SAMA and LAMA classes then selectively block M3 in order to decrease stimulation of bronchoconstriction in the airways. There are certain muscarinic antagonists that also block M2, but the net effect is still the reduction of bronchoconstriction.

SAMAs, especially ipratropium is only used to give quick relief and is often given in combination with longer working beta-agonists. The LAMAs are used to improve baseline lung function and reduce risk of exacerbation.

They may both give dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention etc.

Most SAMAs and LAMAs end in tropium

The drug methachoine used in in bronchial challenges for asthma diagnosis does the reverse. It is a selective muscarinic agonist!

Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS)

Because this class of medication prevents inflammation, it not only improves lung function but also prevents structural airway changes.

The most commonly used ICS are: Budesonide, Fluticasone and Beclomethasone

Systemic corticosteroids are sometimes given in pulmonology in the context of acute inflammatory disorders and certain interstitial lung diseases!

Combination treatment and standard regiments

SABA monotherapy

The most common monotherapy to aid quickly with exacerbations is salbutamol also known as Ventolin. Especially for exercise-related asthma, this is often enough.

SABA/SAMA

When the SABA monotherapy does not help enough, one may switch to a SAMA/SABA combination therapy to combat stronger exacerbations. This includes the highly common ipratropium/salbutamol also known as combivent.

LAMA/LABA

When the exacerbations become too frequent, or when general lung function starts to decline, one may switch to a more sustainable therapy. Only if monotherapy LAMA or LABA doesn't work, one may switch to a combination. One example is a LAMA/LABA such as tiotropium/olodaterol also known as spirolto.

ICS/LABA

When this is still not enough, one may add an ICS. A common example is fromoterol/beclomethason or Foster.

Triple therapy

A combination of a LAMA/LABA and ICS is the last resort. Here we find options such as beclomethason/formoterol/glycopyrronium or Trimbow.

Standard regiments

We start with SABA monotherapy, then add a SAMA if monotherapy is not enough. If exacerbations persist, we add a LAMA or LABA. If a single one of those doesn't help with the complaints, we add the other to form a LAMA/LABA duo. If those aren't enough, we may add an ICS in the form of an LABA/ICS. If that is not enough, triple therapy is the last resort.

As B comes before M, so the SABA/LABA always comes before SAMA/LAMA

Other inhaled medication

Other types of inhaled medication are commonly used in the context of cystic fibrosis. Such medications include mucolytica and inhaled antibiotics.

Mucolytica

To thin the mucus that is characteristicly viscous in cystic fibrosis, we may start with inhaled hypertonic saline which draws water into the aiways and thus thins the mucus. Otherwise, there exists the option of dornase alfa which is an enzyme - gotten through genetic mutation - which breaks down the DNA in mucus, thus reducing its viscosity.

Inhaled antibiotics

As patients with cystic fibrosis often experience pulmonary infections, they might be placed under maintenance therapy. While the most common example is azytromicin per os, another common example is inhaled tobramycin. Note that this is especially useful against the common P. Aeruginosa infections in this patient population.

CFTR-modulators

Another medication used in cystic fibrosis that is not inhaled, are the CFTR modulators which correct the defective CFTR protein which causes cystic fibrosis. Famous examples are ivacaftor, tezacaftor and elexacaftor.

Medications to solve CF end in -caftor
