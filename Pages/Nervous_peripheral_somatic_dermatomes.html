Derma- and myotomes

As we've learned, the spinal cord is divided into roots: 8 cervical nerves, 12 thoracic nerves, 5 lumbar nerves and 5 sacral nerves. Each afferent nerve carries information from a certain area of skin, while each efferent nerve carries information to certain muscles. These concepts are called derma- and myotomes respectively.

Dermatomes

As stated before, dermatomes are those areas of skin served by the same afferent nerve root (i.e., an outgoing nerve, taking information to the brain). Understandably, these kind of images are challenging and hard to memorise. Luckily, a standard list of "landmarks" (i.e., standard locations where a certain dermatome is tested) are defined by the American Spinal Cord Injury Association (ASIA). These landmarks are the following:

  1. C2: 3 fingers behind the ear
  2. C3: In the supraclavicular fossa
  3. C4: Acromioclavicular joint
  4. C5: Radial side of the antecubital fossa
  5. C6: Dorsal surface of the proximal phalanx of the thumb
  6. C7: Dorsal surface of the proximal phalanx of the middle finger
  7. C8: Dorsal surface of the proximal phalanx of the little finger
In anatomical position, the landmarks go from top to bottom, lateral to medial

Myotomes

Our muscles are innervated by nerves, which are intern supplied by various nerve roots. However, specific muscles are used to test specific nerve roots. These are defined by the same organiation as mentioned above.

Upper extremity

For the upper extremity, we could test the following movements:

  1. C1/2: Neck flexion & neck extension
  2. C3: Lateral neck flexion
  3. C4: Elevation of the shoulder
  4. C5: Abduction of the shoulder
  5. C6: Flexion of the elbow & extension of the wrist
  6. C7: Extension of the elbow
  7. C8: Extension of the thumb & ulnar deviation
  8. T1: Finger abduction

Lower extremity

For the lower extremity, we could test the following movements:

  1. L2: Hip flexion
  2. L3: Knee extension
  3. L4: Ankle dorsiflexion
  4. L5: Dig. 1 extension
  5. S1: Ankle plantar flexion
  6. S2: Knee flexion
  7. S3/4: Anal wink reflex
Knee flexion is done by the same root as popliteal fossa innervation!
